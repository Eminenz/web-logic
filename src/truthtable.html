The Boolean connectives in descending order of precedence are

  • ~ for negation,
  • & for conjunction,
  • | for disjunction,
  • => for implication, and
  • <=> for biimplication.
Some examples for formulae are
  • ~A => B | (C <=> A) which is equivalent to ((~A) => (B | (C <=> A))),
  • A & B & C => D which is an example of a Horn-formula and equivalent to (A & (B & C)) => D.
